Proposition 2.1.20.

Equip \(\mathbb{R}^n\) with the dot product and the standard orientation. If \(I = \{i_1, \ldots , i_k \}\) and \(J = \{j_1, \ldots, j_{n - k} \}\) are two disjoint sets whose union is \(\{1, \ldots, n\}\) then
\begin{equation*} \star \left( \mb{e}_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge \mb{e}_{i_k} \right) = \pm \mb{e}_{j_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge \mb{e}_{j_{n - k}}. \end{equation*}
In particular, \(\star\) preserves inner products so that
\begin{equation} \langle \star \tau_1 , \star \tau_2 \rangle = \langle \tau_1, \tau_2 \rangle .\tag{2.1.5} \end{equation}

Proof.
