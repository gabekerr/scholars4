Proposition 3.1.5.

Suppose \(V\) is an \(n\)-dimensional vector space and \(\delta_1 , \ldots, \delta_n\) a basis of the dual space \(V^*\text{.}\) Then the vector space \(\mathcal{L}^k (V)\) is \(n^k\)-dimensional with basis
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{B} = \left\{ \delta_{i_1} \otimes \cdots \otimes \delta_{i_k} : 1 \leq i_j \leq n, \text{ for } 1 \leq j \leq k \right\}. \end{equation*}

Proof.

To see that this is so, one first takes \(\mb{v}_1, \ldots, \mb{v}_n\) to be the dual basis of \(\delta_1 , \ldots, \delta_n\text{.}\) To see that it \(\mathcal{B}\) spans \(\mathcal{L}^k (V)\text{,}\) suppose \(\eta\) is a \(k\)-form. Consider the \(k\)-form
\begin{equation*} \nu = \sum_{(i_1, \ldots, i_k)} \eta \left( \mb{v}_{i_1}, \ldots, \mb{v}_{i_k}\right) \delta_{i_1} \otimes \cdots \otimes \delta_{i_k} \end{equation*}
where the sum is over all \(k\)-tuples of numbers between \(1\) and \(n\text{.}\) It follows then that, for any tuple \((i_1, \ldots, i_k)\) we have
\begin{equation} \nu \left( \mb{v}_{i_1}, \ldots, \mb{v}_{i_k}\right) = \eta \left( \mb{v}_{i_1}, \ldots, \mb{v}_{i_k}\right).\tag{3.1.1} \end{equation}
But then, using the fact that these are multilinear forms, it follows that \(\nu = \eta\) so that \(\eta \) is in the span of \(\mathcal{B}\text{.}\) The linear independence of \(\mathcal{B}\) is left as an exercise.
