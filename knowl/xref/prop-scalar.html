Proposition 4.2.3.

Let \((X, \vol^X)\) be smooth oriented \(m\)-dimensional subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) \(D\) an domain in \(\mathbb{R}^m\text{,}\) \(\mb{G} : D \to X\) an orientation preserving parameterization given by
\begin{equation*} \mb{G} (u_1, \ldots, u_m) = \left( x_1 (u_1, \ldots, u_m), \ldots, x_n (u_1, \ldots, u_m) \right) \end{equation*}
and \(f : X \to \mathbb{R}\) a scalar function on \(X\text{.}\) Let
\begin{equation*} \xi_i = \tder{}{\mb{G}} \left({\partial_{u_i}} \right) \end{equation*}
be the tangent vector field along \(X\text{.}\) Then the scalar integral
\begin{equation*} \int_X f \vol^X \end{equation*}
equals
\begin{equation} \idotsint_D f\left( x_1 (u_1, \ldots, u_m), \ldots, x_n (u_1, \ldots, u_m) \right) \, \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \tder{}{u_1} \cdots \tder{}{u_m}.\tag{4.2.1} \end{equation}

Proof.

To see this proposition holds, simply note that \(\left\{ \xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \right\}\) is a basis for the tangent space at \(X\) (at any given point). This follows from the definition of parameterizations. Taking \(\xi_i^*\) to be the dual cotangent vector, it is an exercise to show that
\begin{equation*} \mb{G}^* \xi_i^* = \tder{}{u_i}. \end{equation*}
Now, since both \(\delta_1, \ldots, \delta_m\) and \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\) are oriented bases for \(T_p X\) with the former an orthonormal basis, and since \(\bigwedge^m T_p X\) is \(1\)-dimensional, we have that
\begin{equation*} \xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m = \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \delta_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \delta_m . \end{equation*}
The dual statement to this is that
\begin{equation*} \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \xi^*_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi^*_m = \, \delta^*_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \delta^*_m = \vol^X. \end{equation*}
Thus we obtain the formula for the pullback of \(\vol^X\) relative to \(\mb{G}\)
\begin{align*} \mb{G}^* \vol^X \amp = \mb{G}^* \, \left( \delta^*_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \delta^*_m \right), \\ \amp = \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \mb{G}^* \left( \xi^*_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi^*_m \right), \\ \amp = \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \mb{G}^* \xi^*_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \mb{G}^* \xi^*_m , \\ \amp = \|\xi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \xi_m \| \, \tder{}{u_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge \tder{}{u_m}. \end{align*}
Putting this into the formula for the integral of an \(m\)-form on \(X\) gives the result.
