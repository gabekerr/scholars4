Example 2.3.3. .

Letâ€™s verify that the volume of a three dimensional ball \(B\) of radius \(R\) is indeed what we think it is. Here
\begin{equation*} B = \{ (x,y, z) : x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \leq R^2 \} \end{equation*}
can be rewritten as
\begin{equation*} B = \left\{ (x,y, z) : (x,y) \in D, - \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} \leq z \leq \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} \right\} \end{equation*}
where \(D\) is the disc of radius \(R\text{.}\) Applying our theorem then gives
\begin{align*} \text{Vol} (B) \amp = \iiint_B \, \tder{}{V}, \\ \amp = \iint_D \, \left( \int_{- \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} }^{\sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2}} \, \tder{}{z} \right) \, \tder{}{A}, \\ \amp = \iint_D 2 \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} \,\tder{}{A}. \end{align*}
We have now reduced a triple integral to a double integral and can continue using methods from last section and a \(u\)-substitution \(y = \sqrt{R^2 - x^2} \cos u\text{.}\) To save some space, we write
\begin{equation*} I = \iint_D 2 \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} \,\tder{}{A} \end{equation*}
and see
\begin{align*} I \amp = 2 \int_{-R}^R \int_{-\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}}^{\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}} \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - y^2} \,\tder{}{y} \, \tder{}{x}, \\ \amp = 2 \int_{-R}^R \int_{\pi}^{0} \sqrt{R^2 - x^2 - (\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}\cos u)^2} \, \left(-\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}\sin u \right)\,\tder{}{u} \, \tder{}{x}, \\ \amp = 2 \int_{-R}^R \int_{0}^{\pi} (R^2 - x^2) \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 u} \, \sin u \,\tder{}{u} \, \tder{}{x},\\ \amp = 2 \int_{-R}^R \int_{0}^{\pi} (R^2 - x^2) \sin^2 u \,\tder{}{u} \, \tder{}{x}, \\ \amp = \int_{-R}^R \int_{0}^{\pi} (R^2 - x^2) (1 - \cos 2 u )\,\tder{}{u} \, \tder{}{x}, \\ \amp = \int_{-R}^R \pi (R^2 - x^2)\, \tder{}{x}, \\ \amp = \pi \left. \left( R^2 x - \frac{x^3}{3} \right) \right|_{-R}^R, \\ \amp = \frac{4 \pi R^3}{3}. \end{align*}
