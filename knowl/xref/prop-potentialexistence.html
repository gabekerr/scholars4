Proposition 5.3.11.

Suppose \(\mb{F}\) is a vector field defined on a simply connected open set \(U\) of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) Then \(\mb{F}\) is conservative if and only if it satisfies the cross-partials test.

Proof.

By Proposition 5.3.2 and Proposition 5.3.9 we need only show that if \(\mb{F}\) satisfies the cross-partials test then it is path independent. Suppose \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) are two smooth paths from \(\mb{a}\) to \(\mb{b}\) in \(U\text{.}\) If they intersect, we may break them up into shorter paths where they do not (or replace one of them over small intervals by a new set of paths). So let us assume they only intersect at the endpoints. Then, reversing the orientation of \(C_2\) we may glue these two paths together to obtain a simple oriented closed curve \(\mathcal{C} = C_1^+ \cup C_2^{-}\) (here the sign in the exponent indicates a change in orientation). Since \(U\) is simply connected, there is a smooth map \(\mb{G} : D \to U\) which sends the boundary \(S^1\) of \(D\) to \(\mathcal{C}\text{.}\) But then, using the fact that \(\mb{F}^\vee\) is closed since it passes the cross-partials test and Stokes’ Theorem 5.2.7 we obtain
\begin{align*} \int_{C_1} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{r}} - \int_{C_2} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{r}} \amp = \int_{\mathcal{C}} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{r}}, \\ \amp = \int_{\mathcal{C}} \mb{F}^\vee , \\ \amp = \int_{S^1} \mb{G}^* \mb{F}^\vee ,\\ \amp = \int_{\partial D} \mb{G}^* \mb{F}^\vee , \\ \amp = \int_{D} \tder{}{\left(\mb{G}^* \mb{F}^\vee \right)} , \\ \amp = \int_{D} \mb{G}^*\left( \tder{}{ \mb{F}^\vee} \right), \\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}
Thus
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_1} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{r}} = \int_{C_2} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{r}} \end{equation*}
and \(\mb{F}\) is path independent.
