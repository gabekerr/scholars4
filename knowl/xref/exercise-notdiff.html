Exercise 7.

Consider the function
\begin{equation*} f(x,y) = \frac{xy}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} \end{equation*}
and define \(f(0,0)\) to equal \(0\text{.}\)
(a)
Show that \(f\) is continuous at \((0,0)\text{.}\)
(b)
Show that \(\pd{f}{x}\) and \(\pd{f}{y}\) are both well defined everywhere (in particular they are well defined at \((0,0)\)).
(c)
Prove that \(f\) is not differentiable at \((0,0)\text{.}\)
