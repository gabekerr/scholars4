Lemma 1.7.12.

If \(V\) is a vector space over \(K\) and \(\nu_0, \nu_1, \ldots, \nu_k\) are dual vectors, then
\begin{equation*} \ker \nu_1 \cap \ker \nu_2 \cap \cdots \cap \ker \nu_k \subseteq \ker \nu_0 \end{equation*}
if and only if there exists \(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_k\) in \(K\) with
\begin{equation*} \nu_0 = \lambda_1 \nu_1 + \cdots + \lambda_k \nu_k . \end{equation*}
