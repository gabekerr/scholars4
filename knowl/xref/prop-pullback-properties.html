Proposition 3.3.8.

Let \(D\) be a domain in \(\mathbb{R}^m\text{,}\) \(E\) a domain in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) and \(\mb{G} : D \to E\) is a differential function. If \(\nu\) and \(\eta\) are differential forms on \(E\) then
  1. \(\displaystyle \mb{G}^* (\eta \wedge \nu) = (\mb{G}^* \eta ) \wedge (\mb{G}^* \nu )\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \mb{G}^* \, \tder{}{\eta} = \tder{}{\mb{G}^* \eta } \)
