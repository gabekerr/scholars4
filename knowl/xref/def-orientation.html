Definition 2.1.14.

An orientation on an \(n\)-dimensional real inner product space \(V\) is a choice of unit vector \(\vol\) for \(\bigwedge^n V\text{.}\) If \(V\) comes with such a choice, we call \(V\) an oriented vector space. We say that
\begin{equation*} \vol_n = \mb{e}_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \mb{e}_n \end{equation*}
is the standard orientation of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
