Exercise 7.

Let \(\mathcal{S}\) be the sphere
\begin{equation*} x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = R^2 \end{equation*}
of radius \(R\) centered at the origin oriented outwardly and
\begin{equation*} \mb{F} = - \frac{GMm}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{3/2}} \left( x \partial_x + y \partial_y + z \partial_z \right). \end{equation*}
be the gravitational vector field between two point masses. Without parameterizing or using spherical coordinates, compute the vector surface integral
\begin{equation*} \oiint_{\mathcal{S}} \mb{F} \cdot \tder{}{\mb{S}}. \end{equation*}
